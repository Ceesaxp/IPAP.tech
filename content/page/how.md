Most consulting wants to become part of your furniture. We plan our exit from day one. A standard engagement is twelve weeks, broken into three honest phases &mdash; and we&rsquo;ll tell you in week two if we&rsquo;ve already learned enough to leave early.

<div class="editorial" style="margin: 4rem 0;">

<article class="editorial-block">
  <div class="numeral" aria-hidden="true">1</div>
  <div class="body-wrap">
    <h3>The reality check.</h3>
    <div class="body">
      <p><em>What we believe:</em> you already have most of the answers. They&rsquo;re buried under politics, assumptions and daily firefighting &mdash; not missing.</p>
      <p><em>What we do:</em> talk to the people the slide deck didn&rsquo;t include. Map what&rsquo;s actually broken vs. what people <em>think</em> is broken. Write the version of the situation everyone privately suspects but no one has yet put on paper.</p>
      <p><em>What you get:</em> a clear picture of what&rsquo;s really happening &mdash; not what the quarterly report says.</p>
      <p class="label-mono" style="margin-top: 1.5rem;">Weeks 1&ndash;2</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</article>

<article class="editorial-block">
  <div class="numeral" aria-hidden="true">2</div>
  <div class="body-wrap">
    <h3>The unleashing.</h3>
    <div class="body">
      <p><em>What we believe:</em> your people want to excel. They&rsquo;re trapped in broken systems and unclear expectations &mdash; not in absence of talent.</p>
      <p><em>What we do:</em> build roadmaps that actually hold up. Set metrics that mean something. Remove the obstacles your team didn&rsquo;t think they were allowed to remove. Coach leaders through real decisions, not table-top exercises.</p>
      <p><em>What you get:</em> immediate visible motion. Team morale shifts inside the first month. Things start shipping.</p>
      <p class="label-mono" style="margin-top: 1.5rem;">Weeks 3&ndash;8</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</article>

<article class="editorial-block">
  <div class="numeral" aria-hidden="true">3</div>
  <div class="body-wrap">
    <h3>The hand-off.</h3>
    <div class="body">
      <p><em>What we believe:</em> the best transformations are self-sustaining. If we&rsquo;re still needed in month six, something earlier in the engagement went wrong.</p>
      <p><em>What we do:</em> transfer knowledge instead of dependency. Build the internal capability that carries the work after we leave. Document the operating model the team now owns.</p>
      <p><em>What you get:</em> an independent, high-performing team that knows where it&rsquo;s going &mdash; and a phone number it doesn&rsquo;t need to call.</p>
      <p class="label-mono" style="margin-top: 1.5rem;">Weeks 9&ndash;12</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</article>

## What you should expect from us

<div class="editorial-card">
  <h3>Speed over politics.</h3>
  <p>Decisions in days, not quarters. Politics slows everything down &mdash; we cut through it. Politely the first time.</p>
</div>

<div class="editorial-card">
  <h3>Inside-out solutions.</h3>
  <p>External best practices rarely fit your context. We design with the talent and constraints you actually have, not with the talent some other company actually had.</p>
</div>

<div class="editorial-card">
  <h3>Real transparency.</h3>
  <p>We&rsquo;ll say the things your own team is afraid to say &mdash; to your team, to your board, and to you. Including when we&rsquo;re the wrong people for the job.</p>
</div>

<div class="editorial-card">
  <h3>Exit-first design.</h3>
  <p>Every milestone is measured against one question: are we closer to not being needed? The answer is supposed to be <em>yes</em>.</p>
</div>
